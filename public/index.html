POPEYES

Company Popeyes

Role Lead Designer

Practice Graphic Design

I worked on the UK launch of the fast-food brand, Popeyes. This involved social content, launch activations and promotions. The goal was to develop a unique brand voice for the UK audience while aligning with the global brand identity. In addition to the branding work, I also led for a graffiti mural activation in Shoreditch, London. The mural was to promote the launch and a Megan Thee Stallion collaboration and a QR code was incorporated into the art, to be scanned for a promo deal. The artwork was created from existing assets given by the Popeyes marketing team. A QR code was incorporated into the mural, allowing passers-by to scan it for a free promo deal.

TURNING TIDE

Company London Festival of Archetecture

Role Designer

Practice Graphic Design

The turning tide was a conceptual bench designed as part of a national competition run by the London festival of Architecture. I and my brother collaborated on the winning design which we were then commissioned to build and install within a month.The theme of the design was "act". Our design interpreted this theme by focusing on the organic transition between mental states, and symbolising the transformative power of rest and reflection on the mind. We wanted the design to portray are fluid change in thought, often the precursor to an act. To connect this idea with the community, we incorporated cages on the sides of the bench, allowing park-goers to leave messages on rocks for future visitors.
